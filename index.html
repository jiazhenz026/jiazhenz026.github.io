About

I'm a Bioengineering Ph.D. candidate at the University of Pennsylvania, working in the Shen Lab. My research centers on optical microscopy instrumentation and metabolomics—from building optical setups and writing acquisition and analysis code to turning raw signals into readouts we can interpret at the level of single cells.

Alongside that, I'm deeply curious about AI for Science and keep a close eye on the latest developments in AI, always looking for ways to put new tools to work on real research problems.

At heart I'm a tinkerer and a geek—when I'm not in the lab I like building small things. This website is one of them.
